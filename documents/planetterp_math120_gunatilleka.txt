Course: MATH120 - Elementary Calculus I
Prerequisite: 1 course with a minimum grade of C- from (MATH112, MATH113, MATH115). Or must have math eligibility of MATH120 or higher; and math eligibility is based on the Math Placement Test.
Restriction: Not open to students majoring in mathematics, engineering, the biological sciences, biochemistry, chemistry, or the physical sciences.
Credit only granted for: MATH120, MATH130, MATH136, MATH140, or MATH220.
Formerly: MATH220.
Basic ideas of differential and integral calculus, with emphasis on elementary techniques of differentiation and applications.

Review 1:
Danul is the GOAT. This course was very easy, especially if you already know some calc from high school. He recorded all the lectures and explained concepts multiple times to make sure everyone understood. Exams were straightforward and followed the structure of the study guides he posted almost exactly. Definitely recommend!

Review 2:
Extremely easy course. There are weekly quizzes in discussion sections and these quizzes genuinely might've been the easiest quizzes I have ever taken. Midterms and final weren't too hard either. Can't really attest to the prof's capabilities since lectures' participation isn't mandatory, but I can tell you MATH120 taught by Gunatilleka is one of the freest courses ever.

Review 3:
I took calc in high school so this was a breeze for the most part. Lectures are boring and slow but the TA's do a good job and the reviews they put out are similar to exams. solid class, he does care but I barely paid attention in lecture and just learned it on my own for what I didn't remember.
