Course: ECON200 - Principles of Microeconomics
Professor: Stefanie Scandizzo

Review 1:
Not the most engaging lecturer, and sometimes gets caught up in examples, but overall explains concepts fairly well. The textbook is also great to read before class, so lecture is not even essential. She does offer polling extra credit which can be received (up to +2% to your final grade) just for showing up and answering polls in lecture, so I would recommend. If you can teach yourself most of ECON, it should be an easy A. She offers plenty of practice and her exams are fairly straightforward, all mcq.

Review 2:
This class felt a lot harder than it needed to be. She just summarizes the smartbook assignments in lecture, really no point in going as you can get all of the information from the smartbook. The exams were hard in my opinion, all multiple choice and some questions straight from the practice exam. However, I studied for exams for 40+ hours, went to gss twice a week, went to tutoring, and still failed. It is a big class and with no discussion it can be hard to get a better understanding of content. I would recommend taking the other professor, as their class was offered better materials and set up for success.

Review 3:
Pretty straightforward class, helpful lectures, exams are fair enough. However, the lectures are extremely dry and boring, so you really need to work to pay attention. There is a lot of homework and reading on McGraw Hill that I feel did not always correspond to what was on the exams or in the lectures.
